? Problem & Reality

? Mismatch between industry needs and formal degrees.
Theory vs. Execution: There is a big gap between the deep theory taught in a 4-year degree and the fast, "action-heavy" needs of the modern Tech and Pharma industries.
Rapid Tool Changes: In global companies, the tools and technologies change every 6 months, while college degrees remain the same for 4 years.
The "Re-learning" Problem: Students graduate with good bookish knowledge but don't know how to handle live production or real-world lab rules.
Not Ready for Work: Even after graduating, students often have to start learning from scratch to meet actual industry standards and compliance.

• Training starts only in pre-final or final years.
Focus on Exams, Not Skills: In most colleges, students spend their first three years only studying books and preparing for exams.
Starting Too Late: Important job skills like  Communication Skills, and Training for Interviews only start in the final or pre-final year.
The Big Gap: Because training starts so late, students don’t get enough time to become experts before they graduate.
Missed Opportunities: By the time students start learning real-world skills, they have already lost three very important years.

• Parents pay twice (degree + extra courses) while students lose 4–5 critical years.
Hard-Earned Savings: Parents spend their life savings on college fees with the simple hope that it will lead to a good job.
Paying Twice: Because degrees focus only on theory, parents are forced to pay a second time for private "job-guarantee" courses after graduation.
Wasted Years: Students spend 4–5 critical years in college but still don't feel ready for the real world.
Starting from Zero: Instead of starting their career after college, students have to start learning basic job skills from scratch.
Financial Stress: This system puts a huge financial load on families while delaying the student's ability to earn.
